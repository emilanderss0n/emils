I've been working with Blender for over a decade, and my workflow has evolved a lot.

## Modeling

I start every project with reference images. Whether it's a hard-surface model or an organic character, having good references makes all the difference.

My modeling approach depends on the project:

- **Game assets** — low-poly with baked detail
- **Renders** — subdivision surface modeling
- **Concept art** — block-out first, then refine

## Texturing

I use Substance Painter for most texturing work, but Blender's built-in tools have come a long way. The key is understanding PBR materials and how light interacts with surfaces.

## Rendering

Cycles is my go-to renderer. I love the realism you can achieve with proper lighting setups. A good HDRI environment map does 80% of the work.
